Top 10 Tips for Choosing Baby Clothing and Accessories

Do you happen to have a baby girl or maybe a baby boy on the way? Like many new parents, you probably want the best of what life can offer them.

For many parents, it begins with the basics: safety, security, and well-being, regardless of age. Don’t worry though, Beibamboo has got you covered.

Choosing clothes for your child may seem like a daunting task, but that is far from the truth. Your baby girl  or  baby boy  can now have a unique style, much like you and your style.

The biggest most significant difference is you’re picking the clothes your baby will wear. It’s also likely there are three things you are looking for in choosing baby clothing—ease of use, long-lasting child-wear that expands and grows with your baby, and protection from the elements indoors and outdoors.

How to Buying Baby Clothes

As many of us already know, babies come in all shapes and sizes; and they can grow-quickly. So, it makes good sense that baby and children’s clothes should come in a range of sizes and be available in a range of colors and easy-to-use.

Whether you’re deciding on bodysuits ( onesies ), sleepers, pants, or hats, wouldn’t it be wonderful if those clothes grew with them—extending the life of the outfits you choose to expand as your infant begins to grow into toddler clothes?

The only task that may slow your decision is the variety, selection, clothing styles, and colors. The possibilities are endless. All you have to do is keep a handful of things in mind.

Here are the top ten tips for selecting baby clothes and accessories.

1. Do You Know the Gender?

If you’re still deciding on baby names, like,Emma,Finn,Monica,Andy, or some other name while you await your baby’sdate of birth —anxiously awaiting to be surprised by their gender, it’s best to go with gender-neutral garments and accessories. So, when it’s time to register for thebaby shower, make sure you’ve selected gender-neutral clothing colors.

2. White Clothes are No Longer Scary

It’s no secret that kids are the messiest. That is why many parents stay away from light colors—white is not your worst enemy.

Stains are not impossible to get rid of when clothes are white and will not react chemically, unlike dye-colored clothes. But when it comes to white or pale colors, you add some chlorine bleach to the stain and wash the baby clothes in hot 60-degree water.

White also pairs well with other select styles of clothes and accessories for mixing and matching the latest trends in baby fashion.

3. Adjustable Clothes are Your Best Friend

Children are always growing, and newborns often outgrow their clothes in a month or so. You may be used to wearing the same clothes you wore a year ago, but your child will never be able to fit into them.

Walking into a store knowing your child will outgrow their clothes in a matter of months can be frustrating, so looking for adjustable clothing that expands and grows with your child will help stretch that dollar further. When it comes to your baby’s clothes, shopping smart can mean the difference in the price range from a low price to the actual price.

Gravitating toward fancy new arrivals will cost you more; outfits that are adjustable, neutral, and long-lasting give your little one an adorable nordic style that is also durable enough to last a long-time.

4. Fancy Outfits are a Big No

There’s nothing wrong with fancy costumes that may seem enticing and cute, but it’s far more affordable to keep those to a minimum. Keep those big brand label outfits for special occasions, and instead opt for neutral colored onesies, rompers, trousers, and hats for everyday adventures for you and your baby or toddler.

The simpler you keep it, the more options and ease you’ll have. It’s fun to dress your child up but always make sure you don’t walk into stores expecting to walk out with ribbons and buttons. The simpler your child’s clothes are, the easier the clothes will be to care for and fit right for longer-lasting clothes.

5. Not Sure About Your Child’s Size and Fit

One-size fits all size isn’t always for everyone, and basing clothes on age or weight isn’t always a good fit since children at different ages are different weights and heights. But you don’t have to worry.

Some brands offer clothes that have adjustable sizes and sell them based on height. It makes things much more manageable, as measuring your child’s height is far simpler than weighing them.

6. Comfort Comes Before Fashion

Fancy outfits in the name of kids’ fashion may seem promising but remember that comfort always comes first. Be sure to pick soft fabrics, like fabric made from bamboo fiber and the stitching that isn’t itchy or poking out. Your child may look cute in that new collections from popular brands, but if the material is itchy or scratchy, he or she will be in tears the entire time or just itching to get out of the clothes.

Look for clothes with seams and labels on the outside. It will give you the option to pick out those clothes with fancy frills without worrying about rashes or itches.

7. Keep the Season In Mind

Young children tend to get very cold in winters and hot in summers. Keeping them in the wrong kind of clothes could cause them to get sick. Ensuring you’re dressing your child according to the weather is critical when it comes to selecting baby clothes for preemies, infants, and children sensitive to a changing climate.

The best way to choose baby clothes is to read the labels. To be completely comfortable, children’s clothes are 50% bamboo viscose and 50% organic cotton. Making the right selection ensures your child feels cool in summer, warm in winter and can play an essential role in how long clothes last.

8. Hygiene Comes First

Babies and toddlers have weakened immune systems, so it is essential to make sure that what they wear is entirely hygienic. Hot water is one of the easiest ways to sanitize their clothes.

Find baby clothes that can withstand high-temperature washers and dryers. Choose clothes made from a material unaffected by harsh chemicals such as bleaches, ammonia, or germ-killing detergents.

9. Choose Washable Clothes

Many times, we end up picking clothes that look nice but are impractical. These clothes include those that are not washable. Be sure to check the tag and make sure all your child’s clothes are washable by washing machines and hand before purchasing them.

The best clothes are washable at 60 degrees, which is the optimum time to sanitize clothes entirely and provide a safe atmosphere for your child.

10. Keeping it Simple

Fancy clothes and accessories may seem enticing, but the golden rule is simplicity. This idea could save you from any issues that come with fancy and uncomfortable clothes. If this is all too confusing for you, here are some things you should look out for shopping for baby clothes:

  • Wide necked or straps around the neck—help keep your children from feeling itchy in the neck area and remove any possibility of the neck being too small.

 

  • Zippers and snaps in the front—clothes with zippers or snaps are much more comfortable to put-on and take-off. You’ll not need to worry too much about your child sitting still.

 

  • Loose sleeves—are simpler to pull off and on. It will take you much longer to deal with tight sleeves, and they may be uncomfortable for your child.

 

Bonus Tip

Make sure to keep an eye out for labels that mention the use of formaldehyde or heavy metals. Some manufacturers have claims of being organic and sustainable. But to be a genuinely sustainable manufacturer, it begins on the farms—just something else to keep in mind while shopping and comparing a similar item, whether in-store or online.

In Conclusion

Parenting can be challenging, but with this information, the decision is more straightforward. Whether you’re shopping for baby girl clothing or toddler clothing for your son, it is much simpler than you think. As long as you’re keeping comfort, hygiene, and safety in mind, you’ll have peace of mind knowing they are comfortable and happy.

The tips mentioned above will help you solve any issues you may have when buying clothes for your little one. Although there are many tips for baby clothes and accessories, it is crucial to keep in mind these tips help prevent skin problems and discomfort.
